	\begin{abstract}
	    The importance of optical fiber communications has become more widespread during the last years because of their features. An increase in data traffic and speeds produces new challenges in this field and leads to the necessity of engendering more developments \cite{Winzer:18}. Apprentices may need to understand profoundly the diverse concepts surrounding this area; here is where E-learning gains importance. This thesis presents the different theories for the creation of a tool capable of fulfilling these necessities. We discuss a few techniques to solve the equations (e.g., the Split-Step Fourier Method) and display the information using Python and Dash/Plotly.  Lastly, we evaluate the best possibility for handling the application to the final users, and we acknowledge the necessity to receive feedback from the students to improve the service.
	\end{abstract}
